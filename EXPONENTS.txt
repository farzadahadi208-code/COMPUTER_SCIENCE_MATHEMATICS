LAWS OF EXPONENTS:
1-EACH NUMBER RAISED TO 1 EQUALS ITSELF.
EX:54^1=54
2-EACH NUMBER EXPECTING 0 RAISED TO 0 EQUALS 1.
EX:45435^0=1
3-IF A NUMBER RAISED TO EXPONENT AND THAT EXPONENT RAISED TO ANOTHER EXPONENET WE CAN MULTIPLY EXPONENTS.
EX:3^3^2 EQUALS 3^(3x2).
4-IF WE HAVE TWO  EXPONENETS NUMBER ARE BEING MULTIPLIED WITH EACH OTHER AND 
HAVE SAME BASE AND DIFFERENT EXPONENTS,WE CAN ADD EXPONENTS.
2^3 TIMES 2^4= 2^(3+4).
5-IF WE HAVE TWO  EXPONENETS NUMBER ARE BEING DIVIDED WITH EACH OTHER 
AND HAVE SAME BASE AND DIFFERENT EXPONENTS,WE CAN MINUS EXPONENTS.
EX:2^3/2^2=2^(3-2).
6-IF TWO NUMBER ARE BEING MULTIPLIED AND HAVING COMMON EXPONENETS THAT EXPONENTS BELONGS TO BOTH.
EX:(2x3)^2= 2^2x3^2.
7-IF TWO NUMBER ARE BEING DIVIDED AND HAVING COMMON EXPONENETS THAT EXPONENTS BELONGS TO BOTH.
EX:(2/3)^2= 2^2/3^2.
